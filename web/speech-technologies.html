The ASR task & how we score it

Word Error Rate (WER) is the yardstick

# reference vs model output
REF: please send my bank statement now
HYP: please send a   balance statement

# 1 sub + 1 ins + 1 del = 3 errors
WER = 3 / 6 = 50%
  • WER = (substitutions + insertions + deletions) / words.
  • Lower is better; 0% = perfect.
  • The single metric that governs every model choice here.
  • Always measured on your audio, not a generic benchmark.

Three ways to decode — the core mechanism

CTC vs. attention vs. transducer

CTCper-frame voting
Connectionist temporal classification: emits a token per audio frame, with a “blank” to handle alignment, then collapses repeats.
fastnon-autoregressiveno language model
Attentionencoder–decoder
A decoder writes text token-by-token, cross-attending to the audio. This is Whisper.
accuratebuilt-in language modelautoregressive
Transducerstreaming family
A recurrent-neural-network transducer (RNN-T) emits text as audio arrives — no waiting for the end of the clip.
streaminglow-latencytrickier to train

Wrap & Q&A

Listen, and speak — on our terms

Mirror images of one problem: STT listens, TTS speaks — and we adapt both to our voices.

What we covered

  • The mirror: STT listens, TTS speaks
  • STT: CTC / attention / transducer
  • TTS: frontend → acoustic → vocoder

Three takeaways

  • Pick STT by latency vs. accuracy
  • Naturalness = acoustic model + vocoder
  • Adapt to our languages; measure on our data
