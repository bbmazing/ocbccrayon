A tiny words list first

How to read a metric

  • Score = a grade (usually higher is better). Rate = how often (usually lower is better).
  • @k = “in the top k results” — e.g. Recall@5 looks at the top 5.
  • p95 = the slow end — 95 out of 100 are faster than this. It catches the bad cases.
  • Red ★ = the metric teams reach for most in real life.

A picture to remember

Recall vs precision — the fishing net

PPrecision = a clean net
almost all you pull up is wanted
rightrightright
caught few, but all correct
RRecall = a full net
you caught most of the fish there
rightrightrightjunkjunk
caught all the right ones, plus junk
Wider net → more caught (recall up) but more junk (precision down). Catch the answer without the junk.

Good answer vs bad answer

Is there a transfer fee?

!Made-up reply

a number the facts never gave — a hallucination

Troubleshooting guide

Which number do I look at?

MetricWhat it means (plain words)
★ Mishears the caller→ Word Error Rate (STT)
★ Can't find the answer→ Recall@k (Retrieval)
★ Makes things up→ Faithfulness (Answer)
★ Leaks private data→ PII leak rate (Safety)
★ Too slow→ p95 latency (Production)
★ Customers upset→ CSAT & repeat-contact (Production)
A symptom → the stage → the metric to check first.
