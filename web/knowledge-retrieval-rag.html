What is an embedding

Text becomes a vector of meaning

  • An embedding maps a whole chunk of text to one vector — a list of numbers.
  • Similar meaning → similar vector → close in space.
  • Retrieval is just comparing these vectors.
"early withdrawal penalty"
      ↓ embed
[ 0.07, -0.21, 0.88, 0.13,
  -0.04, 0.42, ... , 0.19 ]
# up to 4096 numbers

Why not just keyword search

Match meaning, not exact words

Keyword search
Matches exact words. Misses synonyms and paraphrases.
"early withdrawal"≠"breaking your deposit early"
Same meaning, no shared words → no match.
Semantic (embeddings)
Matches on meaning, however it's phrased.
paraphrase ✓synonym ✓cross-language ✓
Catch: can miss exact IDs — fixed later with hybrid.

The Goldilocks problem

Too big, too small, or just right

Too big
whole section in one vector
Meaning diluted by off-topic text → weaker matches.
Just right
one idea per chunk
Focused vector → the paragraph that answers retrieves cleanly.
Too small
a single sentence
Loses the context needed to answer the question.

Strategy 1 · Fixed-size

Cut every N tokens — the blunt baseline

  • Split every 256–512 tokens, ignoring meaning.
  • Simple, fast, predictable sizes.
  • But it cuts mid-sentence and through tables.

Strategy 2 · Recursive

Split along natural boundaries

  • Split by sections → paragraphs → sentences until it fits.
  • Chunks stay coherent — the pragmatic default.
  • Start at ~256–512 tokens, 10–20% overlap.
§ Eligibility — full clean section
§ Interest & payout — full clean section
§ Early withdrawal — full clean section

Strategy 3 · Semantic

Cut where the meaning shifts

  • Embed sentences; cut where meaning changes.
  • Most coherent chunks — but variable sizes.
  • Costs an extra embedding pass — benchmark first.
eligibility + tenure (topic A)
interest + payout (topic B)
penalties + fees (topic C)

Overlap & metadata

Overlap stops orphans; metadata enables citations

  • Overlap (10–20%): share text across boundaries so key sentences aren't split off.
  • Keep metadata per chunk: source, section, page, effective date.
  • Essential for citations and audit in a bank.

Recap

Three things to remember

  • The spine: chunk → embed → store · embed → hybrid search → rerank → generate.
  • Defaults: recursive ~256–512 tokens, 10–20% overlap, contextual + hybrid + rerank.
  • One model, one space (Qwen3-Embedding-8B) — always keep citations.

Take these away

  • Chunking is the highest-leverage knob.
  • Hybrid beats pure-vector search.
  • Always measure retrieval.
